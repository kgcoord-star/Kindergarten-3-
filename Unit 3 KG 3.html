Mathematics

  • 📊 Addition Meanings
  • Joining — combine two groups and find the total

  • Recognise that families around the world keep different types of pets
  • Compare pet customs in Thailand 🇹🇭, Japan 🇯🇵, and ASEAN countries

" Where does our food come from, and how do farms help our community and the world? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise and produce /f/ and /l/ sounds in farm-context CVC words (fog, fun, lip, log)
  • Read and write sight words: out, be
  • Listen to and respond to Topsy and Tim at the Farm by identifying farm animals and their products
  • Write a sentence about a farm animal and what it gives us (e.g. The cow gives us milk.)

Mathematics

  • 📊 Addition Strategies
  • Counting on — start at the larger number and count up
  • Doubles — use doubles facts to add quickly (e.g. 4+4=8)
  • Near doubles — use a known double to solve (e.g. 4+5 = 4+4+1)
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Solve addition problems using efficient strategies
  • Choose and apply the best strategy for a given problem

Science

  • Continue sunflower observation log — record growth changes (sprout, stem, leaves)
  • Identify parts of a plant: roots, stem, leaves, flower, seed
  • Understand that plants grow from seeds and need sunlight, water, and soil
  • Connect plant growth to the food we eat (grains, vegetables, fruits from farms)

Social Studies

  • Understand the farm-to-table journey: planting → growing → harvesting → eating
  • Create a class chart: Farm Animal | Product (Cow→Milk, Chicken→Eggs, Sheep→Wool)
  • Compare farming practices in Thailand 🇹🇭 (rice paddies) with farms in other ASEAN countries
  • Discuss how farmers care for living things and protect the environment

Arts & Crafts

  • Create a 3D habitat diorama representing where an animal lives
  • VA.K.Cr2.1 — Create three-dimensional representations of living environments
  • VA.K.Cn11.1 — Connect art-making to understanding of living things and their habitats

" How are all living things connected, and what happens when one part of a habitat is harmed? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise double consonant ss (miss, toss, mess) and s=/z/ in everyday words (is, has, as)
  • Read and write sight words: have, where
  • Read and discuss what a habitat is — connect to story Squirrels in Antarctica
  • Write a sentence describing an animal in its correct habitat

Mathematics

  • 📊 Three Addends
  • Add three numbers by grouping two that make 10
  • Apply commutative and associative properties to simplify
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Solve addition problems with three numbers
  • Record solutions clearly: (2 + 8) + 5 = 15

Science

  • Define habitat and identify the four things all animals need: food, water, shelter, space
  • Match animals to their correct habitats: desert, ocean, arctic, forest, rainforest
  • Create a physical food chain using animal cards and yarn (sun → plant → insect → frog → bird)
  • Explain why an animal cannot survive in the wrong habitat

Social Studies

  • Explore ecosystems found in Southeast Asia: rainforests, mangroves, rivers, coral reefs
  • Identify animals unique to Thailand and ASEAN 🌏 (elephant, gibbon, clownfish, hornbill)
  • Discuss how human actions (pollution, deforestation) affect habitats and food chains
  • Begin a class 'Protect Our World' pledge wall with one action each student commits to

Arts & Crafts

  • Sculpt garden creatures using clay with texture and surface detail
  • VA.K.Cr2.2 — Use sculpting and clay materials expressively
  • VA.K.Cr3.1 — Refine a three-dimensional artwork with attention to texture and detail

Weekly Inquiry Question
"What materials make up our Earth, and how do people help take care of them?"
ELA · Phonics
  • Recognize and produce the unvoiced th sound (think, three, thumb)
  • Read and spell want and good as sight words
  • Identify th words in shared reading texts
  • Write th words using letter tiles and whiteboards
ELA · Language
  • Identify key Earth vocabulary: rock, sand, soil, hard, soft
  • Understand information from non-fiction read-aloud
  • Respond to text questions using simple sentences
  • Begin sentences with capital letters and end with periods
  • Write simple sentences about Earth materials with drawing
Math · 2D Shapes
  • Identify and name: circle, square, rectangle, triangle, hexagon
  • Describe shapes by number of sides and corners
  • Sort and compare shapes by their attributes
  • Find 2D shapes in the natural environment
Science
  • Identify common Earth materials: sand, soil, rocks
  • Explore materials using senses (touch, sight)
  • Describe where each material is found (beach, garden, mountains)
  • Record observations using pictures and simple labels
Social Studies
  • Identify community helpers who care for the Earth
  • Understand roles of gardeners, cleaners, and park workers
  • Demonstrate respect and appreciation for these roles
  • Connect community helpers to personal responsibility
Arts & Crafts
  • Design a poster communicating a message about caring for the Earth
  • Use colour and text to share an environmental message
  • Connect art to environmental stewardship
  • Share ideas visually with peers

📅 Week 28 — Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
📅 Week 28|🏫 KG3 · Age 5|🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Environmental Responsibility — Reduce, Reuse, Recycle|🔍 Who We Are
💛Value:Responsibility & Sustainability
🤝CASEL:Responsible Decision-Making
📖Phonics:Digraph: th (voiced) · Sight Words: away, down
🧘Mindfulness:Breathing — inhale for the trees, exhale pollution
🎯Approach:Inquiry-Play Based Learning
Weekly Inquiry Question
"How can the choices we make every day help protect our Earth?"
ELA · Phonics
  • Distinguish voiced th (this, that, them, the) from unvoiced th
  • Read and spell away and down as sight words
  • Sort voiced/unvoiced th words using picture cards
  • Write sentences using th words and sight words
ELA · Language
  • Learn vocabulary: reduce, reuse, recycle, waste, environment
  • Understand a non-fiction read-aloud about recycling
  • Describe how each action (reduce/reuse/recycle) helps the Earth
  • Write 'I can help the Earth by ___' with illustration
  • Use adjectives to describe materials (plastic, paper, glass)
Math · 3D Shapes
  • Identify and name: sphere, cube, cylinder, cone, rectangular prism
  • Describe solid shapes by faces, edges, and vertices
  • Sort and compare 3D shapes by their attributes
  • Connect 3D shapes to everyday recycled materials
Science
  • Sort materials into categories: recycle, reuse, reduce, landfill
  • Understand why some materials take longer to break down
  • Explore what happens to different materials in soil
  • Make predictions about decomposition
Social Studies
  • Identify how communities reduce waste
  • Understand the role of recycling centres and composting
  • Connect personal actions to community well-being
  • Begin to see themselves as responsible citizens
Arts & Crafts
  • Create a sculpture using only recycled and repurposed materials
  • Use creativity to see new possibilities in everyday waste
  • Explore how materials can be repurposed sustainably
  • Present and explain their creation to peers

📅 Week 29 — Living Things and Our Earth
📅 Week 29|🏫 KG3 · Age 5|🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Environmental Responsibility — Living Things and Our Earth|🔍 Who We Are
💛Value:Compassion & Sustainability
🤝CASEL:Social Awareness & Self-Management
📖Phonics:Digraph: ng · Sight Words: help, make
🧘Mindfulness:Growing meditation — imagine growing from a seed into a tree
🎯Approach:Inquiry-Play Based Learning
Weekly Inquiry Question
"How do plants and animals depend on a healthy Earth to survive and grow?"
ELA · Phonics
  • Read and spell words ending in ng: ring, sing, king, swing, long, strong
  • Read and spell help and make as sight words
  • Blend CVC + ng words fluently in decodable sentences
  • Use ng words in speaking and simple writing
ELA · Language
  • Identify what plants need: sunlight, water, soil, air
  • Understand what animals need to survive in their habitats
  • Use action words (verbs) to describe living things: grow, drink, eat, breathe
  • Write 'Plants need ___. Animals need ___.' with detailed illustration
  • Listen and respond to non-fiction text about ecosystems
Math · Composing Shapes
  • Combine 2D shapes to build composite shapes (e.g. triangle + square = house)
  • Decompose shapes into their component parts
  • Analyse what shapes make up pictures of living things
  • Use pattern blocks to build animals and plants
Science
  • Explore what plants need to grow (soil, water, sunlight, air)
  • Begin a class planting experiment — observe daily
  • Identify living vs. non-living things in the environment
  • Record plant observations over time in a science journal
Social Studies
  • Learn how some cultures have cared for the Earth for generations
  • Discuss indigenous and traditional environmental practices
  • Connect respect for living things to personal values
  • Share family traditions of caring for plants or animals
Arts & Crafts
  • Weave with natural and fibre materials to explore texture and pattern
  • Observe texture and pattern in natural objects (leaves, bark, grass)
  • Explore the artistic traditions of weaving across cultures
  • Describe textures and patterns seen in their work

📅 Week 30 — Water, Weather and Our Promises
📅 Week 30|🏫 KG3 · Age 5|🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Environmental Responsibility — Water, Weather and Our Promises|🔍 Who We Are
💛Value:Gratitude & Protection
🤝CASEL:Self-Awareness & Responsible Decision-Making
📖Phonics:Vowel Team: ai (long A) · Sight Words: yes, no
🧘Mindfulness:Water meditation — imagine a cool, clean stream flowing peacefully
🎯Approach:Inquiry-Play Based Learning
Weekly Inquiry Question
"Why is clean water essential for all living things, and what promise can I make to protect it?"
ELA · Phonics
  • Recognise the long a vowel team ai: rain, sail, tail, main, wait, paid
  • Read and spell yes and no as sight words
  • Read ai words in decodable sentences and books
  • Spell ai words using magnetic letters and whiteboards
ELA · Language
  • Learn vocabulary: water cycle, evaporation, rain, cloud, river, ocean, conserve
  • Ask and answer questions using: What? Where? Why? How?
  • Write a personal promise: 'I promise to protect the Earth by ___'
  • Connect weather patterns to water in the environment
  • Listen to and respond to a non-fiction book about water
Math · Fractions
  • Partition circles and rectangles into halves and fourths (quarters)
  • Understand that equal parts must be the same size
  • Use the words half and fourth/quarter accurately
  • Apply fractions to share and divide real objects fairly
Science
  • Explore the water cycle: evaporation, condensation, precipitation
  • Identify sources of water: rain, rivers, oceans, groundwater
  • Understand the importance of clean water for living things
  • Discuss simple water conservation strategies
Social Studies
  • Learn about weather patterns and how they affect people globally
  • Understand that some communities lack access to clean water
  • Begin to develop empathy for people in different environments
  • Connect personal water use to global responsibility
Arts & Crafts
  • Create personal artwork that communicates a commitment to protecting the Earth
  • Use imagery, symbols, and colour to express an environmental promise
  • Connect personal values and feelings to visual expression
  • Reflect on the meaning behind their artwork

Science

Social Studies
  • Learn about young environmental changemakers (e.g. Greta Thunberg, Wangari Maathai)

  • Reflect on personal strengths as an Earth guardian
  • Develop a class Earth Guardian pledge
Arts & Crafts
  • Collaborate to create a large-scale mural representing shared responsibility for the Earth
  • Contribute a personal element to the collective artwork
  • Connect art to community action and shared values
  • Present and explain the class mural to peers

  • Write an artist's statement: 'This artwork shows ___. I made it by ___. It means ___.'

  • Use unit vocabulary to talk about Earth, sustainability, and growth
  • Listen to and appreciate peers' presentations respectfully
Math · Review & Application
  • Review 2D and 3D shapes found in artwork and the environment
  • Apply fractions in art display: half the board, a quarter of the gallery
  • Solve real-world exhibition problems: How many artworks? How many visitors?

  • Reflect on all Earth science concepts learned in Unit 6
  • Share science journal observations with visitors and classmates
  • Connect class planting experiment to understanding of living things
  • Celebrate growth of plants alongside personal growth
Social Studies · Reflection
  • Reflect on how the unit changed their understanding of their role on Earth
  • Celebrate collective achievements as a class community
  • Thank and honour peers, teachers, and families for the learning journey
  • Connect learning to being a responsible and caring member of society
Arts · Exhibition
  • Prepare, display, and present a year's worth of artwork to families and visitors
  • Write an artist's statement for each displayed piece
  • Reflect on artistic growth from Unit 1 to Unit 6
  • Connect learning and personal experiences to their creative work

📅 Week 6: Exploring the Seasons 👧 Grade: KG3 (Age 5) 🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Seasonal Awareness & Curiosity 🌍 Transdisciplinary Theme: Who We Are
🌱 Curiosity & Collaboration — I wonder about the world and explore it together with others
CASEL: Social Awareness · Self-Awareness  ·  Phonics: Review — blend & segment all taught sounds  ·  Sight Words: my, are  ·  🧘 Mindfulness: Weather Breathing (slow inhale = breeze; long exhale = wind fading)  ·  🎯 Approach: Inquiry-Play Based Learning
🔎 What I Wonder — Weekly Inquiry Question
"What changes in our world when the seasons change?"

🎯 Week 6 Learning Goals — By the End of This Week Students Will:

📖 ELA 1 — Reading & Phonics
  • Blend and segment all previously taught GPC sounds using letter tiles and Elkonin boxes
  • Match letters to initial sounds in seasonal and weather vocabulary words
  • Read decodable CVC words and simple sentences in a seasonal reading context
  • Read and use sight words my and are in independent and shared reading
✏️ ELA 2 — Language, Literature & Grammar
  • Write 1–2 sentences about a favourite season using invented and conventional spelling
  • Label seasonal drawings with weather vocabulary words
  • Use my and are correctly in written sentences
  • Contribute a page to the shared class book "In ___ season, I see…"
🗣 Speaking & Listening (Circle Time)
  • Describe seasonal changes using weather vocabulary in complete sentences
  • Share observations about seasons and weather during morning meeting
  • Ask and answer "What season?" and "What is the weather?" questions with a partner
  • Practise the weekly inquiry question using think-pair-share
🔢 Math — Place Value to 100
  • Identify tens and ones in two-digit numbers using base-ten blocks
  • Build two-digit numbers to 20 using physical and visual materials
  • Count groups of ten objects and write the corresponding numeral
  • Explain how many tens and ones are in a given two-digit number
🔬 Science / STEM
  • Use the five senses to observe and describe seasonal natural objects
  • Sort natural items (leaves, ice, flowers, soil) by season and observable properties
  • Record observations through drawings in a personal science journal
  • Identify one way a plant or animal changes with each season
🌍 Social Studies
  • Name the four seasons and describe a key feature of each
  • Locate and discuss seasonal differences on a world map or globe
  • Identify seasonal celebrations and activities from two or more cultures
  • Connect personal experiences to seasonal changes in the local environment
❤️ SEL (CASEL)
  • Identify and express feelings of curiosity and wonder when exploring new topics
  • Practise turn-taking and active listening during seasonal group discussions
  • Share opinions about favourite seasons respectfully, acknowledging others' views
  • Recognise how curiosity and observation help us understand the world (CASEL: Social Awareness)
🎨 Arts & Crafts
  • 🎨 Four Seasons Tree — Represent seasonal changes through painting
  • Add seasonal elements in four quadrants: spring blossoms · summer leaves · autumn colours · winter snow
  • VA.K.Cr2.1 Represent observations and experiences through drawing and painting
  • VA.K.Cn11.1 Connect personal artwork to observations of the natural world
🎵 Music
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🏃 PHE & Movement
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
💻 Computer
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🌐 World Language
  • Practise weather vocabulary greetings in a second language (Chinese or Arabic) during World Language time
🧠 ATL & Play-Based Learning
  • Thinking: make predictions and ask questions about seasonal changes
  • Research: observe and record findings from nature walks using drawings and labels
  • Communication: share seasonal discoveries using words, drawings, and gestures
  • Self-management: transition independently between season activity centres

📅 Week 7: Wind and Rain 👧 Grade: KG3 (Age 5) 🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Adaptation & Weather Patterns 🌍 Transdisciplinary Theme: Who We Are
🌀 Adaptability — I can change and adjust, just like the weather
CASEL: Self-Management · Resilience  ·  Phonics: g, o — /g/ goat, garden · /o/ octopus, top  ·  Sight Words: he, with  ·  🧘 Mindfulness: Wind Breath (inhale 4 · exhale 6 like a fading gust)  ·  🎯 Approach: Inquiry-Play Based Learning
🔎 What I Wonder — Weekly Inquiry Question
"How do wind and rain change the way we dress, play, and live?"

🎯 Week 7 Learning Goals — By the End of This Week Students Will:

📖 ELA 1 — Reading & Phonics
  • Recognise and produce the /g/ sound in initial position (goat, game, gap)
  • Recognise and produce the /o/ short vowel sound (octopus, top, fog)
  • Blend CVC words containing g and o: got, fog, bog, log, god
  • Read and recognise sight words he and with in context
✏️ ELA 2 — Language, Literature & Grammar
  • Write CVC words with g and o using letter formation on lined paper
  • Write 2 sentences about a rainy day using sight words he and with
  • Draw and label a stormy weather scene with 3+ vocabulary words
  • Contribute a sentence to the class rainy-day journal
🗣 Speaking & Listening (Circle Time)
  • Describe wind and rain using adjectives: loud, soft, strong, gentle, cold
  • Retell the story "The Wind Blew" using sequence words: first, then, finally
  • Discuss how rainy or stormy weather makes them feel and what they do to adapt
  • Practise "he" and "with" in spoken sentences about weather and friends
🔢 Math — Place Value to 100
  • Represent two-digit numbers in expanded form (e.g. 34 = 30 + 4)
  • Write two-digit numbers in standard and expanded form
  • Explain place value: "My number has ___ tens and ___ ones"
  • Match base-ten block representations to written two-digit numbers
🔬 Science / STEM
  • Predict and test which objects are moved by wind force
  • Record results using a simple table with pictures
  • Describe wind as a natural force that can move, push, and change things
  • Connect wind observations to real-world weather patterns (storms, kites, sails)
🌍 Social Studies
  • Identify rainy and stormy climates on a simple world weather map
  • Discuss how people in rainy regions (e.g. Thailand, UK, rainforest) dress and live
  • Compare rainy season in one country with weather at school
  • Recognise that weather affects the activities and clothing choices of communities
❤️ SEL (CASEL)
  • Identify feelings (nervous, excited, cosy) associated with stormy weather (Self-Awareness)
  • Discuss what it means to be brave like Brave Irene when facing difficulty
  • Practise self-calming strategies during the Storm-into-Calm mindfulness activity
  • Adapt behaviour for indoor rainy-day activities: calm voice, careful movements (Self-Management)
🎨 Arts & Crafts
  • 🎨 Weather Texture Board — Explore weather through tactile mixed media
  • Select and apply materials that represent different weather textures (rough, smooth, soft, crinkly)
  • VA.K.Cr1.1 Explore and experiment with a variety of art-making materials
  • VA.K.Re7.1 Observe and describe texture and visual qualities in artwork and nature
🎵 Music
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🏃 PHE & Movement
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
💻 Computer
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🌐 World Language
  • Practise weather-related vocabulary in a second language; storm and rain phrases
🧠 ATL & Play-Based Learning
  • Thinking: predict outcomes of wind experiments before testing
  • Research: observe and record which objects respond to wind using a simple table
  • Communication: describe stormy weather using adjectives and sensory details
  • Self-management: regulate emotions and stay safe during windy outdoor play

📅 Week 8: Hot and Cold 👧 Grade: KG3 (Age 5) 🌐 Global Context: Identities & Relationships — Personal Identity & Belonging + Temperature, Adaptation & Resilience 🌍 Transdisciplinary Theme: Who We Are
❄️ Resilience — I am strong and adaptable in every kind of weather
CASEL: Self-Management · Growth Mindset  ·  Phonics: c, k — /k/ cat, cap · kick, key (same sound!)  ·  Sight Words: me, she  ·  🧘 Mindfulness: Sunshine Warmth Breathing (breathe in golden warmth; feel it spread through the body)  ·  🎯 Approach: Inquiry-Play Based Learning
🔎 What I Wonder — Weekly Inquiry Question
"How do living things keep warm or cool when the weather changes?"

🎯 Week 8 Learning Goals — By the End of This Week Students Will:

📖 ELA 1 — Reading & Phonics
  • Recognise and produce the /c/ (hard c) sound: cat, cap, cut, cold
  • Recognise and produce the /k/ sound and understand c and k share the /k/ phoneme
  • Sort c and k words; read CVC words containing c and k in decodable texts
  • Read and recognise sight words me and she in context
✏️ ELA 2 — Language, Literature & Grammar
  • Write CVC words with c and k using correct letter formation
  • Write 2 sentences comparing hot and cold weather using me and she
  • Label a drawing of an animal adapting to a temperature (e.g. bear in snow)
  • Contribute to the class book "Cold, Cool, Crisp: A Weather ABC"
🗣 Speaking & Listening (Circle Time)
  • Describe temperature using adjectives: hot, warm, cool, cold, freezing, boiling
  • Retell key events from "The Snowy Day" using beginning, middle, and end
  • Discuss how animals and people adapt: hibernation, migration, clothing, shelter
  • Use she and me in spoken sentences about weather activities
🔢 Math — Place Value to 100
  • Compare two two-digit numbers using base-ten blocks and number lines
  • Use >, <, and = symbols correctly when comparing pairs of numbers
  • Solve comparison word problems using seasonal temperature contexts
  • Order a set of three two-digit numbers from smallest to greatest
🔬 Science / STEM
  • Predict and test how temperature affects the rate of melting of ice
  • Design a simple insulation solution using available materials
  • Explain how animals adapt to cold (fur, hibernation, migration) or hot (shade, burrowing)
  • Record experiment results using drawings and simple labels
🌍 Social Studies
  • Identify hot and cold climate regions on a world map (Arctic, Sahara, tropics)
  • Describe how people in different climates dress and build their homes
  • Compare Thailand's hot season with cold regions; discuss similarities and differences
  • Recognise that people adapt their lifestyles to temperature and climate
❤️ SEL (CASEL)
  • Identify what it feels like to be resilient when something is hard or uncomfortable
  • Discuss times they have adapted and shown strength (like animals in cold weather)
  • Practise staying calm during challenging activities using breathing strategies
  • Recognise that both hot and cold emotions are natural; choose how to respond (Self-Management)
🎨 Arts & Crafts
  • 🎨 Rainy Day Print Patterns — create repeating weather-inspired patterns
  • Select printmaking tools (sponges, forks, caps) to represent rain, hail, and puddles
  • VA.K.Cr2.2 Use printmaking tools to create artwork
  • VA.K.Re7.1 Identify visual patterns and texture qualities in artwork
🎵 Music
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🏃 PHE & Movement
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
💻 Computer
  • Weekly activities — see specialist teacher
🌐 World Language
  • Practise hot and cold vocabulary in a second language; temperature-related phrases
🧠 ATL & Play-Based Learning
  • Thinking: predict experimental outcomes and explain reasoning before testing
  • Research: collect temperature evidence using observations and simple instruments
  • Communication: report findings from temperature experiments using drawings and labels
  • Self-management: stay resilient when an experiment does not go as predicted

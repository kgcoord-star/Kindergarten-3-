" How has life changed from long ago to today, and what can we learn from the past? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise and produce the unvoiced /th/ digraph: moth, bath, thin, thank, three
  • Read and write sight words: want, good
  • Read anchor text Then and Now — identify differences between past and present life
  • Use time vocabulary: long ago, today, then, now in oral sentences
  • Write and illustrate: 'Long ago, people ___. Today, people ___.'

Mathematics

  • 📊 Measuring Length
  • Measure objects using non-standard units (cubes, hands, paperclips)
  • Measure with standard tools (ruler, metre stick)
  • Compare lengths: longer, shorter, same
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Measure objects using non-standard and standard tools
  • Compare lengths using appropriate vocabulary

Science

  • Explore how technology has changed over time — mystery bag activity
  • Compare old and modern objects: rotary phone vs. smartphone, VHS vs. streaming, Walkman vs. Bluetooth
  • Discuss: How does new technology make life easier?
  • Create a 'Past vs. Present Technology' T-chart with drawings and labels

Social Studies

  • Introduce Unit 5 theme: Travel Through Time — Past, Present, and Future
  • Discuss: What does 'long ago' mean? What clues can we find in old pictures and objects?
  • STREAM Fair celebration week — sharing and presenting our learning
  • Begin a class 'Time Travel Journal' — draw or write one thing that was different long ago

  • Cave Painting Adventure — create art inspired by ancient cave painting styles and storytelling

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise and produce the voiced /th/ digraph: this, that, they, them, then, there
  • Distinguish between voiced /th/ (this) and unvoiced /th/ (thin) — tongue placement
  • Read and write sight words: away, down
  • Listen to Stone Age Boy and retell how the Stone Age family lived
  • Write: 'Long ago, families ___. Today, families ___.' — compare then and now

Mathematics

  • 📊 Time
  • Read an analog clock — hour hand and minute hand
  • Tell time to the hour (3 o'clock)
  • Tell time to the half-hour (half past 3)
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Tell time to the hour on analog and digital clocks
  • Tell time to the half-hour on analog and digital clocks

Science

  • Explore how Stone Age people used tools, fire, and natural materials
  • Compare Stone Age tools to modern equivalents: flint knife → metal knife, cave → house
  • Discuss: Why did people live in caves? What materials did they use and why?
  • Draw and label a 'Stone Age Invention' — what would you create if you had only rocks and sticks?

Social Studies

  • Note: Makha Bucha Day — school holiday observed this week 🇹🇭
  • Explore how Stone Age families gathered food, made shelter, and lived together
  • Compare Stone Age family roles to family roles today
  • Create a 'Families Then and Now' comparison drawing — what stayed the same?

  • Then and Now Collage — compare past and present through a collage connecting personal and historical images

" How has the way we move and travel changed over time, and how might we travel in the future? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise the /ai/ vowel team — rain, mail, nail, aim, train, sail
  • Identify the long /a/ sound in the 'ai' pattern: r-ai-n, m-ai-l
  • Read and write sight words: yes, no
  • Read spelling sentences: 'Yes, I can aim. The rain is here. There is no mail. My nail is red.'
  • Use adjectives to describe transport: fast, slow, big, loud, old, new

Mathematics

  • 📊 Data and Graphing
  • Collect data using a class survey
  • Create a picture graph using tally marks
  • Interpret graphs — most, least, equal, more than, fewer than
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Collect and organise data into a picture graph
  • Interpret graphs to answer comparison questions

Science

  • Explore push and pull forces using a basket on wheels (vehicle simulation)
  • Demonstrate: push from behind vs. pull using a rope — how does the basket move?
  • Identify how wheels make objects easier to move — try sliding vs. rolling
  • Connect to transport: all vehicles use push or pull forces to move

Social Studies

  • Compare transport from the past and present: horse and cart, ship, train → car, plane, rocket
  • Begin preparing for International Day — learn about a country (Russia, India, or China) 🌍
  • Explore famous landmarks of the country represented: flag, traditional clothes, food, buildings
  • Start creating a craft or decoration for International Day classroom display

Arts & Crafts

  • Future City Design — imagine and design a city of the future using creative materials
  • VA.K.Cr1.1 — Generate imaginative ideas about what future cities might look like
  • VA.K.Cr2.1 — Develop and create a design using available materials

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise and produce the /ee/ vowel team: see, bee, meet, sleep, green, tree
  • Read and write sight words: after, again
  • Read spelling sentences: 'See you again. There is a bee. After I sleep. Good to meet you.'
  • Listen to Mechanimals and generate creative ideas for future inventions
  • Write and illustrate 'If I Built a Car…' narrative (beginning, middle, end)

Mathematics

  • 📊 Measurement & Data Projects
  • Apply measuring length, time, money, and graphing skills
  • Solve real-world project tasks combining multiple skills
  • Sequence events using a schedule — before, after, first, next, last
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Apply all Unit 5 measurement and data skills in project contexts
  • Read and interpret a daily schedule using sequencing vocabulary

Science

  • Explore robots and machines — how can they help us in the future?
  • Identify tasks robots can do: cleaning, building, helping, cooking
  • Build a simple 'robot' using blocks or recycled materials — what will your robot do?
  • Draw your robot and explain: 'My robot helps by ___'

Social Studies

  • International Day — celebrate the country our classroom represents (Russia, India, or China) 🌍
  • Helping Our Future World — discuss recycling, saving energy, clean Earth
  • Recyclng Sort activity — sort paper, plastic, and rubbish into the correct bins
  • Draw 'The Earth in the Future' — clean parks, recycling robots, solar panels

Arts & Crafts

  • Time Traveler Gallery — prepare and present unit artwork for a gallery; express preferences
  • VA.K.Pr5.1 — Prepare artwork for exhibition with a clear title and explanation
  • VA.K.Re9.1 — Express opinions about artwork using 'I think…' and 'I like… because…'

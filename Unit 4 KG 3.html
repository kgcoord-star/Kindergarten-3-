" Where do building ideas come from, and how can we turn our ideas into something real? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise and produce /y/ (yet, yak, yell) and /z/ (zip, zap, zoo) sounds
  • Read and write sight words: come, here
  • Listen to The Most Magnificent Thing and identify how the character gets and changes her ideas
  • Express a building idea orally and in writing: 'My idea is to build ___'

Mathematics

  • 📊 Meaning of Subtraction
  • Taking away — combine two groups and remove
  • Part-part-whole — understand what's left
  • Comparing — find the difference between two groups
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Model subtraction situations using manipulatives and pictures
  • Represent subtraction with equations (e.g. 7 − 3 = 4)

Science

  • Explore basic physics: build a ramp for a toy car using blocks and cardboard
  • Test the ramp and observe how the car moves — faster/slower, far/near
  • Identify variables: What makes the car go further? (angle, surface, height)
  • Record observations with drawings and one written sentence

Social Studies

  • Identify community helpers who build: architects, engineers, construction workers
  • Discuss what buildings look like in Thailand 🇹🇭 — temples, homes, schools, markets
  • Compare types of homes in different ASEAN countries: stilts, concrete, wooden
  • Connect to inquiry: How do builders in our community make ideas into real things?

Arts & Crafts

  • Create an Inventor Sketchbook — generate and record invention ideas through drawing and planning
  • VA.K.Cr1.1 — Generate and explore artistic ideas through imaginative drawing
  • VA.K.Cr2.1 — Develop and organise artistic plans through sketching

ELA / Phonics

  • Read and write sight words: little, big

  • Write: 'My box is not a box. It is a ___' — creative writing with illustration

Mathematics

  • 📊 Fact Families
  • Addition-subtraction relationships: 3+4=7, 4+3=7, 7−3=4, 7−4=3
  • Use a fact family triangle to see connections
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Understand addition and subtraction as inverse operations
  • Identify and complete fact families within 20

Science

  • Explore structures: what makes a building tall and stable? (base, weight, balance)
  • Build the tallest tower possible using blocks, cups, or recycled materials
  • Test stability: does the tower fall? What went wrong? How can we fix it?
  • Introduce engineering design process: imagine → plan → build → improve

Social Studies

  • Discuss famous structures students know: Eiffel Tower, temples in Thailand, the Burj Khalifa
  • Explore ASEAN landmarks: Petronas Towers (Malaysia), Angkor Wat (Cambodia) 🌏
  • Connect to inquiry: Why do people build tall, beautiful, or special structures?
  • Draw and label 'My Dream Structure' inspired by global and local landmarks

Arts & Crafts

" How can we design and build things that solve real problems and help our community? "

ELA / Phonics

  • Recognise the /sh/ digraph in initial and final position (ship, shop, shed, fish, dish)
  • Read and write sight words: play, look
  • Listen to What Do You Do with a Problem? and discuss how the character faces and solves challenges
  • Write: 'I see a problem. I will ___' — problem-solution sentence frame

Mathematics

  • 📊 Missing Number Problems
  • Unknown addend: 5 + ? = 9
  • Complete equations with missing numbers: ? − 3 = 4
  • Use addition facts to solve subtraction unknowns
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Solve unknown addend problems using counting on and fact families
  • Complete equations with missing numbers in any position

Science

  • Explore recycled materials: which materials can we reuse to build something new?
  • Design a simple invention to solve a classroom problem (e.g. a book holder, a pencil organiser)
  • Build the invention from recycled materials and test it
  • Reflect: Did it work? What would you change?

Social Studies

  • Discuss how communities solve problems by building (rain shelters, water systems, markets)
  • Explore how Thai engineers and inventors solve problems in Thailand 🇹🇭
  • Compare: How do different ASEAN communities use local materials to solve problems?
  • Create a 'Community Problem + Solution' poster — identify a local problem and draw a building solution

Arts & Crafts

  • Sculpt an original invention using clay with detail and intention
  • VA.K.Cr2.2 — Use sculpting techniques and clay materials expressively
  • VA.K.Cr3.1 — Refine artistic work with attention to texture and form

ELA / Phonics

  • Review and apply all digraphs learned: ch, sh, zz, qu in reading and writing

  • Prepare and present a verbal explanation of a personal creation: 'I built ___ because ___'
  • Listen to peers' explanations and ask one question: 'I wonder why you chose ___'

Mathematics

  • 📊 Subtraction Fluency
  • Recall and apply all Unit 4 subtraction strategies
  • Fluently subtract within 20 with speed and accuracy
  • OBJECTIVES:
  • Fluently subtract within 20
  • Explain the strategy used: 'I used counting back because…'

Science

  • Display and explain all unit science investigations: ramp, materials test, tower, invention
  • Write one discovery sentence for each experiment in the science journal
  • Share: 'I discovered that ___ because ___' — use evidence from observations
  • Discuss: How does science help us design and build better things?

Social Studies

  • Celebrate creativity from Thailand and ASEAN — showcase inspired by real-world builders and inventors
  • Make connections: How do builders in our community use creativity to help people?
  • Reflect on Unit 4: What did I learn about myself as a creative person?
  • Write a Unit Commitment: 'I will use my creativity to ___'

Arts & Crafts

  • Present and explain creative work to an audience at the Maker Fair
  • VA.K.Pr4.1 — Present artwork and creations with confidence and clear explanation
  • VA.K.Re8.1 — Explain artistic choices: why you used those materials and made those decisions
